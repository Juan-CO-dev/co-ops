## Colors

### Primary Palette

Anchor + base colors. Mustard is the anchor; Mayo and Diet Coke are for backgrounds and text.

| Name | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Mustard | `#FFE560` | Anchor color |
| Mayo | `#FFF9E4` | Background / light surfaces |
| Diet Coke | `#141414` | Text / dark backgrounds |

### Secondary Palette

Per the brand book: "focuses on blue and green, with accents to be used sparingly for contrast. Our brand red should be used most sparingly of all — only as a CTA text or where a more eye-catching accent is needed."

| Name | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Blue | `#3F73B9` | Secondary |
| Green | `#28B25C` | Secondary |
| Green Accent | `#9AFFC0` | Accent (sparing) |
| Blue Accent | `#A9CDFF` | Accent (sparing) |
| Red | `#FF3A44` | CTA text / eye-catching accent ONLY — most sparingly |

### Color usage rules for the wordmark

- Wordmark should **only ever use primary brand colors** on primary marketing materials.
- Secondary palette is acceptable for "moments" — organic social, website, places where the user is already familiar with the brand.

## Typography

### Primary typeface — Midnight Sans

- Used for: headlines, subheads, CTAs (buttons)
- **Always in ALL CAPS**
- Specimen weight referenced: Midnight Sans Rd / 12 Bold

### Secondary typeface — GT America

- Used for: long-form body copy
- Available weights: Light → Bold
- Standard for body: Regular and Medium
- Bold for emphasis

### Note typeface — GT Alpina TW Thin Italic

- Used for: notes / annotations

### Type scale

| Style | Font | Size | Line Height | Tracking | Weight | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| H1 | Midnight Sans | 48px | 56px | -20 | Bold | Primary page headlines, hero sections |
| H2 | (Midnight Sans, implied) | 36px | 44px | -15 | Bold | Section headers, major content divisions |
| H3 | GT America | 24px | 32px | -10 | Bold | Sub-sections, feature headings |
| H4 | GT America | 18px | 24px | 25 | Light | Minor headings |
| Body 1 | GT America Regular | 26 | 24px | 0 | Regular | Highlight content (non-header) |
| Body 2 | GT America Regular | 18px | 20px | 0 | Regular | Main body content, supporting text |
| Note | GT Alpina TW Thin Italic | 18pt | 20pt | -4% | Thin Italic | Notes |

> Note: Body 1 size shown as "26" (no unit) in the source PDF — likely 26px. Body 1 line height (24px) is smaller than the size, which appears to be a brand book error; flag with Pete/Julia before using.

## Logo Variants & Usage

There are 4 variants. The brand book identifies them by these names:

### 1. Wordmark (the PRIMARY logo)

- File: `co-wordmark.png`
- "This is our primary logo because of its clear legibility. It has been customized and so should not be reproduced by typing new text."
- **Use for:** Website headers, official documents, single-color applications where simplicity is required, large-format displays where legibility is paramount.

### 2. Wordmark + Icon (full lockup)

- File: `co-wordmark-icon.png`
- "This iterates on our primary logo for a more decorative option. It maintains some legibility but should be used carefully because the icon becomes less readable when scaled down."
- **Use for:** Primary marketing material, building signage, any first-impression touchpoint, large-format displays.

### 3. Logomark

- File: `co-logomark.png`
- **Use for:** Premium applications, packaging, brand merchandise, environmental graphics.
- **Do NOT use** in places with limited height constraints (website logos, social media thumbnails) — would need to scale down to an illegible size.

### 4. Icon

- File: `co-icon.png`
- **Use for:** Social media avatars, favicon, small-scale applications, watermark, pattern element, simplified brand expressions.
- Same height-constraint warning as Logomark (not for limited-height contexts).

### Universal usage restrictions (apply to ALL variants)

- Never compress, stretch, or alter letter spacing.
- Maintain approved color specifications.
- Do not apply effects, shadows, or gradients.
- Always reproduce from approved digital files — never alter, redraw, or recreate.

## What the Brand Book does NOT specify

Be aware the following are **not** in the brand book — flag with Pete/Julia if you need them:

- **Minimum logo size** (in px or inches). Not specified.
- **Clearspace/safe-area requirements** around logos. Not specified.
- **Light-bg vs dark-bg variants explicitly named** (e.g., no "logo on dark" inverse versions defined). The PNG deliverables don't include dedicated dark-bg variants.
- **Tone-of-voice for written copy.** No "voice / messaging" section in the brand book. The only tone described is photographic (see below).
- **Email-specific guidance.** No email template, no email-safe color or font fallbacks.

## Photography direction (for context, not copy)

**Food / product:** Direct pop-flash, high contrast, deep blacks + bright highlights. Bold, unfiltered, commands attention. Stark lighting transitions, centered straight-on composition. Post-production preserves natural contrast and neutral color temperature — no heavy filters or retouching. "Raw, documentary-style authenticity."

**Environmental:** Authentic, unscripted moments in CO spaces. Natural staff interactions, hands-at-work detail, dynamic group moments, environmental portraits. Journalistic distance. Authenticity over perfection.
